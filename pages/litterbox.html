My Least-Mess, Least-Hassle Litterbox Routine

  1. Bring the kitchen trashcan to the bathroom and place near tub.
  2. Pull litterbox away from the wall and alongside the trashcan. Scoop is behind litterbox, tucked under weird heater thing.
  3. Open litterbox by tilting lid upward 90 degrees and removing lid.
  4. For best results and minimal spillage, sit on tub facing litterbox.
  5. Scoop!
  6. Reverse Steps 1-3.
  7. ...
  8. Profit!

Pee Zones:

  • Big buried clumps in upper left (as seen from tub-seat), fairly easy to get without breaking
  • Sloppy half-buried clumps in upper right and lower right, may take some covering and edgewise finesse scrape-scooping to keep intact-ish

Poop Zones:

  • Anywhere. It's a total minefield!
  • The good thing is that they don't tend to bury it all that deep.

Fresh Litter

If the box starts to get low on litter, there's a bag in the hall closet. Be advised that the litter is very dusty and you may want to avoid breathing it in.

Tips 'n' Tricks:

  • I find that tilting the whole box toward the heater-facing side first makes the upper left pee zone easy to get.
  • It will also help cover more of the unburied pee in the heater-side of the box.
  • Tilting the whole box away from the heater-facing side will then expose the other pee zone and make that easier to get before moving on to the poops.
  • (I have found that scooping the pee zones first will avoid breaking up the clumps while searching for poops.)
